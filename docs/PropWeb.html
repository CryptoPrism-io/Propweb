01 · The Big Picture

What PropWeb is, in one breath

India's trust-first, AI-powered rental platform: verified owners meet verified tenants directly — no brokers, no fake listings, no spam calls. Bayut-grade product quality on a NoBroker-style direct model.

The tenant's pain

Fake listings farmed for leads. One flat listed by 10 brokers at 10 prices. One enquiry = 20–50 broker calls. Dead listings everywhere. 10–20 visits before finding a fit.

The owner's pain

Non-genuine tenants and price-fishers. Phone number and address leaked publicly. Coordinating every single visit alone. Zero visibility into who they're renting to.

The one-liner to remember: "Existing portals sell leads. PropWeb sells trust." Every slide, wireframe and demo screen should serve that sentence.

Who's in the room: two co-founder CEOs funding this personally from their existing profitable company. Yogesh joins as part-time CTO+CPO (20 hrs/week), leads all tech and product, hires the team. The pitch's job: get the CEOs to approve a budget scenario, greenlight the first hires, and sign off a 90-day plan.

02 · The Two References

Bayut and NoBroker — know these cold

Every conversation about PropWeb happens against these two companies. One is our quality benchmark, one is our business-model reference and main competitor.

Bayut (Dubai) — copy the quality

UAE's top property portal (Dubizzle Group). Famous for TruCheck (physically verified listings, geotagged photos), TruEstimate (AI price valuation, mid-to-high-90s% accuracy claims), TruBroker (agent trust scores), BayutGPT (chat search). 600+ engineers, Python/React/AWS/Elasticsearch. We borrow their trust-feature philosophy and UI polish — NOT their broker-based model.

NoBroker (India) — copy the model, fix the flaws

India's first proptech unicorn. Direct owner-to-tenant, freemium contact unlock (plans ₹1,499–₹10,999). FY24: ₹803 Cr revenue, ₹411 Cr loss — spent ₹1.62 to earn ₹1. Trust is enforced by call-centre humans, not tech: fake listings and broker infiltration remain complaint #1. That gap is our opening.

If a CEO asks "why will you win?": NoBroker proved Indians pay to skip brokers, but they do trust with people (₹436 Cr employee cost). We do trust with software — verification pipeline, trust score, broker detection — which is cheaper, scales, and can't be retrofitted by portals built to sell leads.
03 · The Product

Six pillars and one flow

The product story is six pillars. The demo tells it in four steps. Learn both — they're the backbone of your deck's Act 1.

Verified Owner Badge

Aadhaar/PAN + ownership proof + mobile verification.

Verified Tenant Badge

Employment, income range, KYC — owners see who they rent to.

Pay-to-Connect

Browsing free; contacts unlock for a small fee. Kills fake enquiries.

AI Matchmaking

Budget, location, family/bachelor, furnishing — a match score, like matrimony for rentals.

Rental Trust Score

Verification + response rate + freshness + reviews. On every listing.

Intelligence, not leads

An AI engine that recommends, ranks and protects — not a directory reselling contacts.

STEP 1

Verify

Both sides complete KYC and earn badges before transacting.

STEP 2

Match

Engine scores listings against the tenant's real needs.

STEP 3

Connect

Small fee unlocks contact — only genuine intent gets through.

STEP 4

Transact

Trust score + digital rent agreement close the deal safely.

Launch scope: rentals only, Bengaluru first, web app before native apps. Revenue today = connect fees + premium listings + badge subscriptions. Revenue tomorrow = services marketplace (movers, cleaning, furniture, interiors) — NoBroker's own non-listing revenue reached ~50% of its mix, proving services outgrow listings.

04 · The Build Plan

Four phases, gated by numbers

The tech story (deck Act 2). Core idea: a modular monolith web app — one well-organised building, not twenty microservice buildings — built by a lean in-house team, with money released phase by phase only when the previous gate is hit.

  • The legal line you must not fumble: private companies cannot use Aadhaar OTP/biometric eKYC (Supreme Court, 2018). PropWeb uses the legal route — offline Aadhaar XML / DigiLocker / licensed KYC partners.
  • Where the moat is: verification pipeline (8–12 person-weeks), trust score (4–6), broker detection (5–8), matchmaking (5–8). Everything else is commodity assembly.

06 · Your Deliverables

What you're building, and the bar for done

1 · The deck (16–18 slides)

Two acts: product story (slides 1–9), tech + money story (10–16). Follow the solution-pitch markdown slide-by-slide — headlines, key messages and speaker notes are already written. Presenter notes on every slide.

2 · Wireframes (11 screens)

Home/search, results (list+map), listing detail, pay-to-connect modal, tenant onboarding, tenant KYC, owner listing wizard, owner KYC, owner dashboard, trust-score explainer, match-score view. Mobile-first at 390px, then desktop.

3 · Costing slides (2)

Reproduce the budget table (3 scenarios) and hiring sequence + unit economics from Section 05. No new math — just clean design.

4 · Demo web app

Rentals only. Three journeys: tenant search → detail; owner listing wizard → published; trust badges + score everywhere, tappable. Local JSON only (25–30 Bengaluru listings, ₹18k–85k), Leaflet + OpenStreetMap, no backend/DB/auth/payments. One command to run, deployed to a free static link.

  • Design direction: fresh, inspired by Bayut's clarity (whitespace, cards, map-right, calm palette) — do NOT clone it. Where NoBroker feels cluttered and upsell-heavy, PropWeb feels calm and verified. Trust marks are the hero on every screen; every badge is tappable to show what was verified and when.
  • Demo script (what we click live): search Koramangala 2BHK ₹35k → open a 95-score listing → tap badge → Connect → mock pay → masked-number reveal → switch to owner → 4-step wizard → "pending verification" → flip to Verified. Close on the trust-score explainer: "this is the moat."
  • Checkpoints with Yogesh: Day 2 deck skeleton + first sketches · Day 4 all wireframes + app scaffold · Day 7 journeys clickable + deck 80% · Day 9 full dry run · Day 10 lock and deploy.
  • Definition of done: you can explain every slide and screen without Yogesh in the room.
